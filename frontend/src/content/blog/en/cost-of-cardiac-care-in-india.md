India has become one of the most trusted destinations for cardiac care. World-class hospitals, JCI-accredited centres and experienced interventional cardiologists mean patients receive the same (or better) outcomes they would expect at home — at a fraction of the cost.

## What drives the price difference?

In Bangladesh, the UAE or the UK, cardiac procedures can be expensive because of hospital overheads, insurance markups and long waiting lists. In India, economies of scale, competition among top-tier hospitals and a mature medical tourism ecosystem keep prices lower while maintaining high standards.

## Typical starting costs in India

- Coronary angiogram: from $200
- Angioplasty with one stent: from $2,500
- CABG (bypass surgery): from $4,500
- Valve repair or replacement: from $6,000

These are all-inclusive starting estimates. The final quote depends on the hospital city, surgeon experience, stent type, room category and any companion stay.

Yes. Hospitals in our network use the same FDA- and CE-approved devices, follow global infection-control protocols and publish audited outcomes. Many cardiac surgeons have trained and practised in the US, UK and Europe before returning to India.
