Medical tourism can save money and time, but only if your facilitator is transparent, experienced and accountable. Here is what to look for before you commit.

## 1. Verified hospital partnerships

A good facilitator works directly with accredited hospitals, not middlemen. Ask for hospital names, accreditations and whether the coordinator can arrange a direct video consultation with the doctor.

## 2. Clear, written cost estimates

Avoid anyone who gives a single number without a breakdown. Your estimate should include hospital charges, doctor fees, implants, medication, accommodation, transfers and follow-up.

## 3. In-country support

You should have a dedicated point of contact in India who speaks your language, meets you at the airport and is available during your hospital stay — not just a remote call centre.

## 4. Aftercare planning

Recovery does not end when you fly home. Ask how follow-ups, report sharing and emergency escalation are handled after you return.

## 5. No pressure, no false promises

Beware of guarantees like "100% success" or demands for large upfront cash payments. Ethical facilitators explain risks, answer questions and let you decide at your own pace.
